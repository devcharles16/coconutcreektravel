Top 3 Caribbean Island Destinations

  • by Admin
  • 3 min read
  • 20 Comment

During the Winter months, those who live in the Caribbean have no complaints. Why? Because it's always sunny and warm and they are happy. Want to feel the sun, warmth and happiness too? Here are our top 3 Caribbean island destinations to escape the winter coldness.

  • 1. St. Thomas, U.S Virgin Islands
    St. Thomas is one of the three United States Virign Islands. The other 3 are St. Croix, St. John and Water Island. St. Thomas is is usally the most desired island destination on Caribbean cruises. It is well known for it's historical landmarks, beaches and duty free shopping. The temperature all year round is usually around 80 degrees so it's the perfect weather no matter what time of year you visit. Places to visit on this tropical island are:
    • The World Famous Mountain Top
    • Magen's Bay Beach and Drakes Seat Lookout
    • Coral World Aquarium
    • Paradise Point Tramway
    • Havensight Shopping Mall, Vendor's Plaza and Main Street
    Shopping - One of the best things about shopping in St. Thomas is that there are no taxes. So you save a lot when buying high priced items. The other good thing about shopping here is that you can negotiate on just about anything. Watches, purses, jewelry and much more.
    Beaches - Magens Bay is one of the most popular among tourists. There is a restaurant and bar and water activities. Coki Point is also another popular beach right next to the Coral World Aquarium.
    Sightseeing - When you get to the airport or cruise ship dock, you can ask your taxi driver for a tour. They are knowledgeable, funny and more than happy to do it.
  • 2. Dominica
    The island of Dominica (not to be confused with the Dominican Republic) is nick named the the Nature Isle of the Caribbean. It has the world's second-largest hot spring called Boiling Lake and it's rainforests gives it it's luscious green look. Average temperature all year round is usually around 80 degrees as well.
    Places to visit on this tropical island are:
    • Boiling Lake
    • Champagne Reef
    • Trafalgar Falls
    • Carib Cultural Village by The Sea
    • Morne Trois Pitons National Park
    Diving - Dominica is great for snorkeling and diving. Plaves like Champagne Beach, Point Break and Whale Shark Reef are great for experiencing all the diving Dominica has to offer.
    Beaches - Champagne Beach was named that because it has bubbling waters caused by the hot spring beneath it. It is probably the most beautiful beach on the island. Other great beaches are Pointe Baptiste, Mero Beach and Batibou Beach.
    Kalinago Territoy - The descendants of the Caribs which were the first inhabitants of Dominica live in eight villages on this reservation. You will have an opportunity to experience their culture first hand.
  • 3. St. Kitts and Nevis
    The islands of St. Kitts and Nevis are an independent country filled with history. It's a volcanic island so beaches are filled with beautiful black sand. Average temperature here? You guessed it! Usually around 80 degrees.
    Places to visit on this tropical island are:
    • Brimstone Hill
    • Romney Manor
    • Cockleshell Beach
    • Carib Petroglyphs
    • Dieppe Bay Beach
    History - Brimstone Hill is a well preserved fortress. It was designed to protect the coastline from attacks. Experience real military architecture at this historic site.
    Beaches - Not all beaches on this beautiful island have black sand. To experience this beauty, you will need to travel to the northern end of the island to Dieppe Bay.
    Sightseeing - The Scenic railway is a great way to see the island and learn about it's history. Take a train ride through miles of scenery on the island.
